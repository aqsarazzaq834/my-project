Research Proposal

Applied Mathematical Models for Predicting Climate-Induced Disasters

The project aims to develop hybrid modeling pipelines that combine physical process understanding with machine-learning approaches to forecast climate-driven hazards. We will explore time-series analysis, stochastic modeling, PDE-based simulation, and probabilistic risk estimation to improve early-warning and resource planning.

  • Data sources: historical weather, hydrological records, satellite products.
  • Methods: numerical simulation, regression & tree-based ML, sequence models.
  • Outcomes: risk maps, uncertainty bounds, and decision-support dashboards.
